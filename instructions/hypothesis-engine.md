# Hypothesis Engine - Instructions for Claude Desktop / Cowork

## What This Skill Does

Runs the Double Diamond methodology for strategic hypothesis generation and selection. First it diverges -- generating 6-10 candidate hypotheses using 20 thought starters drawn from the showrunner deck. Then it evaluates each on desirability, feasibility, and viability. Then it converges to the top 2-3, developing each into a dual-format hypothesis card with an outcome statement and a validation commitment.

**Time:** 75 minutes
**Track:** Product - Activity 2 (11:00am-12:15pm)

## How to Use

Copy everything below the line and paste it into a new Claude Desktop or Cowork conversation.

---

## Activation Prompt

```
I'm at Founder Jam Product Track working on hypothesis design. Help me run through the Hypothesis Engine using the Double Diamond method.

My startup context:
- Startup name: [your startup name]
- Problem it solves: [one sentence]
- Target customer: [specific -- job title, company size, industry, demographic]
- Stage: [idea / pre-revenue / early-revenue]
- Key competitive findings from my battle card session: [paste your "How We Win" one-liners or top competitive insight]

Run me through these steps:

1. Frame the hypothesis formats -- show me the two templates I'll use:
   - Outcome statement: "If we [change/experiment], then [target customer] will [measurable outcome] by [specific date]."
   - Validation commitment: "We believe [ICP] will pay $[Y]/month for [specific outcome]. We will validate this by [specific experiment] within [timeframe]."

2. Diverge phase -- use the 20 hypothesis thought starters to help me generate 6-10 candidates customized to my startup. Pick the most relevant thought starters based on my stage and problem. Output a diverge board table with all candidates labeled H1 through H[n].

3. Evaluate phase -- score each hypothesis on desirability (1-5), feasibility (1-5), viability (1-5), and risk (1-5). Show a scoring table. Flag anything below 8 total as unlikely to be worth pursuing. Sense-check feasibility against my actual stage and resources.

4. Converge phase -- help me select the top 2-3. For each selected hypothesis, develop:
   - The full outcome statement
   - The full validation commitment
   - Why this one (2-3 sentences)
   - Fastest way to be wrong (1 sentence)
   - Validation experiment brief (one paragraph)

5. Final selection -- ask me to pick one winning hypothesis to take into the Prototype Launcher.

Challenge me throughout:
- "Which hypothesis are you avoiding because it would require the most change? That's probably the most important one."
- "What's the fastest you could know if this hypothesis is wrong?"
- "Are you testing a real risk to the business, or confirming something you already believe?"
- "What would it take to prove this wrong in 2 weeks?"
```

## What to Expect

1. Claude will share the hypothesis formats and anchor you in what makes a good hypothesis
2. You'll generate 6-10 candidates together -- don't self-edit in the diverge phase
3. Claude will score each and surface trade-offs between high-impact and high-risk options
4. You'll get 2-3 fully developed hypothesis cards in dual format
5. You'll choose one winning hypothesis to take into prototyping

## Output Format

- Diverge board: all 6-10 candidates as a Miro-ready table
- Evaluation table with scores and rationale
- 2-3 hypothesis cards in dual format (paste into Miro sticky notes)
- Validation experiment brief per selected hypothesis
- One winning hypothesis statement (save this -- the Prototype Launcher needs it)

## Tips for Better Output

- Bring your battle card output -- knowing your competitive landscape improves hypothesis quality
- The hypothesis you're most uncomfortable with is usually the most important one to test
- If your hypothesis doesn't have a specific number and date, it's not a hypothesis yet -- it's a goal
